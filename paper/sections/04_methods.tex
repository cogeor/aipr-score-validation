\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{The scoring system}
\textsc{AIPR} grades a manuscript in a single frozen pipeline version (denoted
v6; never mixed with other versions in one cohort). The pipeline uploads the PDF
and, in its full configuration, runs a two-pass review: a reviewer pass that
reads the paper and produces the five dimension scores with justifications, and
an audit pass that re-reads the paper with a literature-search tool to verify the
reviewer's claims and surface omitted citations against a real bibliographic
index (so a recommended citation is always a record the tool returned, never a
hallucination). Both study configurations run this same full two-pass pipeline;
they differ only in the reviewer model. The overall score is computed server-side
as a fixed weighted mean of the five dimensions,
\begin{equation}
  \text{overall} = \frac{4\,s_{\text{nov}} + 2\,s_{\text{rig}}
  + 4\,s_{\text{app}} + 1\,s_{\text{cla}} + 0.5\,s_{\text{cit}}}{11.5},
  \label{eq:overall}
\end{equation}
with novelty and applicability carrying the top weight and citation the least;
the model does not emit the overall directly. We treat the system as a fixed
black box: it was not tuned on the venue, the decisions, or the reviewer ratings,
and no model in this study was trained or fine-tuned on the evaluation data. The
weighting was frozen before this study from separate internal calibration and was
not fit to any decision, rating, or outcome used here; we therefore evaluate the
scoring rule \emph{as deployed} and report its sensitivity to the weighting
(equal-weight and leave-one-dimension-out) in Appendix~\ref{app:robust}.

\subsection{Grading configurations and cost design}
Two configurations run the identical full two-pass pipeline and differ only in the
reviewer model (Table~\ref{tab:configs-app}, Appendix~\ref{app:configs}):
\textbf{full-mini} (cheap model) and \textbf{full} (frontier model)---the
production configuration. The cheap-model configuration is effectively
unconstrained in volume; the frontier configuration is budgeted (the realized
per-configuration token cost is reported in Appendix~\ref{app:robust}). We
therefore establish the score--outcome relationship on the large full-mini cohort
and use the frontier cohort to confirm it, after first verifying that the
cheap-model and frontier scores agree well enough for the former to stand in for
the latter (the bridge, hypothesis~H5).

\subsection{Hypotheses, metrics, and pre-registration}
All hypotheses, the primary metric, and the low-score threshold were fixed before
any score was joined to any outcome (Appendix~\ref{app:prereg}). We report effect
sizes with bootstrap confidence intervals throughout, because ``strong signal''
is an effect-size claim, not a $p$-value claim. In the vocabulary of measurement
validation, H1--H4 assemble criterion-related evidence for a specific score
interpretation (weak relative to the venue bar) under a specific use (triage);
validity here attaches to that interpretation and use, following the unified
framework of the \emph{Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing}
\citep{aera2014standards}. A separate pre-registered comparison (V1) asks whether
that validity is a product of the engineered pipeline rather than of the
underlying model---the primary \emph{value} question, on which the case for
building the system rests.

\begin{description}\itemsep2pt
  \item[H1 (primary): low-end flagging.] Submissions in the lowest AIPR-score
  quintile are rejected at a rate well above the base rate, and top-tier (oral)
  papers are essentially absent from that band. Reported as reject rate and as
  \emph{lift} over the base reject rate, with Wilson intervals.
  \item[H2: separation.] The overall score discriminates reject from accept,
  measured by AUROC with a stratified bootstrap CI.
  \item[H3: monotone gradient.] Mean overall score increases monotonically across
  the three ordered tiers, tested by the Jonckheere--Terpstra trend statistic with
  a \emph{Monte Carlo permutation} $p$-value and reported with Spearman $\rho$
  against tier rank.
  \item[H4: continuous agreement.] The overall score correlates with the mean
  reviewer rating (Spearman $\rho$, bootstrap CI).
  \item[H5 (gate): mini-to-frontier bridge.] On cohort $H$, the full-mini and
  frontier overall scores correlate at $\rho \ge 0.8$, licensing the large-$N$
  full-mini results as a proxy for the production (frontier) score.
  \item[V1 (primary value): pipeline vs.\ generic prompt.] On cohort $H$, the full
  pipeline out-discriminates a single one-paragraph ``grade this paper'' prompt run
  on the same model and PDF, with the only variable the pipeline. Pre-declared
  success: AUROC(full)~$-$~AUROC(naive)~$>0$ with the CI excluding zero; reported
  alongside accept/reject agreement at AIPR@60 (and each method at its own
  accept-rate-matched threshold) and run-to-run reliability. A null is reported
  plainly as a negative product result.
\end{description}
Secondary analyses (per-dimension discrimination, whether the audit pass adds
signal, run-to-run variance, prevalence re-weighting, weighting robustness, and the
leakage controls of \S\ref{sec:discussion}; a second-venue replication is deferred
to future work) are reported in
\S\ref{sec:results} and Appendix~\ref{app:robust}. Effect sizes use Cohen's $d$
and Cliff's $\delta$ for the two-group contrast; multiple comparisons across the
five dimensions are controlled by the Benjamini--Hochberg procedure. Confidence
intervals are 4{,}000-resample bias-corrected and accelerated (BCa) bootstraps,
which attain nominal coverage on skewed statistics such as AUROC at the frontier
cohort's sample size (Appendix~\ref{app:power}); AUROC and other class-conditional
statistics use a \emph{stratified} resample that preserves the observed
reject/accept counts. The Monte Carlo trend test uses 10{,}000 permutations. The
entire analysis is deterministic under a fixed seed.
