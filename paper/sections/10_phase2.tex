\section{Phase 2: ICLR 2025 ordinal arm and citation-audit re-validation}
\label{app:phase2}

This appendix reports the pre-registered Phase-2 extension (addendum tag
\texttt{prereg-iclr2026-phase2}): a four-tier ordinal validation arm on the
public ICLR 2025 review record on OpenReview, and a re-validation of the
citation audit after its fix. The v1 findings are unchanged: every v1
reading --- including the criterion-not-met results --- stands as published,
and no v1 number is re-run or revised here.

\subsection{Ordinal validation on the ICLR 2025 record}
\label{app:phase2:ordinal}

ICLR 2025 awards four decision tiers (Reject $<$ Poster $<$ Spotlight $<$
Oral), one tier finer than the 2026 primary venue --- a finer ordinal
discrimination test of the score. On the stratified full-mini cohort
($n=\pTwoN$, \pTwoVenue): accept/reject AUROC \pTwoAurocFull; Spearman
$\rho$ against the mean reviewer rating \pTwoSpearmanRatingFull;
bottom-quintile reject rate \pTwoBottomReject\% (lift
$\pTwoBottomLift\times$ over the base rate). The pre-declared ordinal
additions: Spearman $\rho$ against the four-tier rank
\pTwoSpearmanTierFull; the per-tier median scores are \pTwoMonotone\ along
the ladder (trend test, \pTwoTrendPrel); the adjacent-boundary AUROCs are
reported in Table~\ref{tab:phase2ordinal} and the per-tier distributions in
Figure~\ref{fig:phase2tiers}. Per the addendum, the Poster--Spotlight
boundary CI is reported but not headline-claimed (spotlights are a small
fraction of accepts). Because ICLR 2025 decisions precede the grading
model's training cutoff, this arm is an in-training-window consistency
check; ICLR 2026 remains the clean primary.

\subsection{Citation-audit re-validation (new validation; the v1 result stands)}
\label{app:phase2:pillar1}

frozen grading configuration (pinned at 100, chance discrimination); that
artifact row is frozen and stands unmodified. Phase 2 re-grades the same
frozen cohort-H ids under \texttt{full\_full\_p2} --- the v6 pipeline with
the fixed, abstract-based citation audit; single run per paper --- as a
\emph{new validation} of the fixed pipeline, not a revision of v1. On the
re-graded cohort ($n=\pOneN$): citation pinned-at-100 rate \pOnePinned\%
