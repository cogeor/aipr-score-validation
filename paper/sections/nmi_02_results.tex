\subsection{A first-pass score tracks human outcomes}
The overall score separates rejected from accepted submissions with
AUROC~\aurocMiniFull{} (full-mini), confirmed at \aurocFullFull{} on the frontier
cohort; the standardized accept/reject gap is large (Cohen's $d=\cohensDMini$,
Cliff's $\delta=\cliffsDeltaMini$). Mean score rises monotonically across the three
ordered tiers (Jonckheere--Terpstra \trendPrel; Spearman $\rho=\trendRho$, 95\% CI
\trendRhoCI), and the score correlates with the continuous mean reviewer rating at
$\rho=\spearmanRatingMiniFull{}$ (full-mini) and \spearmanRatingFullFull{}
(frontier). Because the reviewer rating averages over individual reviewer noise, it
is the cleaner test of agreement with human-perceived quality, and the effect is
strong rather than merely significant (Fig.~\ref{fig:validation}).

\subsection{The signal is strongest for low-score triage}
The validated action is allocation of human attention, and the score is sharpest at
the low end where human review is itself most reliable. On the production frontier
model the lowest-score quintile is rejected at \bottomRejectRateFrontier\% (95\% CI
\bottomRejectCIFrontier), a \bottomLiftFrontier$\times$ lift over the
\baseRejectRate\% base rate, with no oral papers in the band; the same pattern holds
at scale on the cheap-model cohort (\bottomRejectRate\%, lift \bottomLift$\times$,
95\% CI \bottomLiftCI{} clearing the pre-registered success rule). The reliability
curve (Fig.~\ref{fig:validation}b) falls monotonically as the score rises, crossing
the base rate near mid-scale. The bottom-quintile threshold is sample-relative, not
an absolute cutoff: it is a venue-level capacity allocation---the venue chooses the
share of submissions to inspect---rather than a universal rejection rule. At the
venue's natural prevalence (\natAcceptRate\% accept), the bottom-quintile reject
precision is \natBottomPrecision\%.

\subsection{A frontier model already carries much of the signal}
\label{sec:res-value}
A frontier model is available to anyone, so we grade the frontier cohort a second
way with a single one-paragraph prompt that asks the same model, on the same PDF,
for an overall quality score---the realistic baseline a researcher would paste into
a chat assistant (verbatim in the SI). The bare prompt already separates rejected
from accepted submissions at AUROC~\aurocNaiveFull. The full pipeline reaches
\aurocFullFull, and the paired difference is small and not statistically resolved
($\Delta$AUROC~\aurocDiffFullNaive, 95\% CI \aurocDiffCI, \aurocDiffPrel;
$n=\naiveN$): we claim neither superiority nor equivalence---the study was not
powered to rule a small gap in or out over an already strong baseline
(Fig.~\ref{fig:naive}a). What the engineering adds is a deployable score, not a
higher one. It grades far more consistently across repeated runs (median within-paper
SD \fullRunSD{} vs.\ \naiveRunSD{} points; Fig.~\ref{fig:naive}b), and the same pass
returns a structured, grounded, rubric-anchored review rather than a bare number.
A fixed-cutoff operating point (AIPR@60) is a diagnostic, not the deployment claim:
because the production score is compressed high near the venue bar, a 60 threshold
predicts almost every paper accept (\opSixtyFullAcc\% vs.\ \opSixtyNaiveAcc\%
balanced accuracy) and is not read as a classifier.

\subsection{Failure modes define the deployment boundary}
\label{sec:failure}
For triage the costly error is an accepted or oral paper landing in the low-score
band. On the frontier score no accepted (\lowAcceptCountFrontier/\nAcceptFrontier)
or oral (\lowOralCountFrontier/\nOralFrontier) paper does; on the larger cohort
\lowAcceptCountMini/\nAcceptMini accepted and \lowOralCountMini/\nOralMini oral
papers do. Read qualitatively (SI), the false negatives are contribution-level
novelty judgments and defects not visible from the PDF; the false positives are
strong papers with real but forgivable concerns, which cost only a second human look.
This asymmetry---cheap false positives, expensive false negatives at the top---is why
the validated use is surfacing weak work for attention, never automated rejection,
and where human review remains non-negotiable. The lightly weighted citation
subscore is reported separately and is \emph{not} validated in this cohort, where an
audit fallback pinned it (AUROC \citationAUROC); dropping it leaves the headline
unchanged (AUROC \aurocDropCitationFull{} vs.\ \aurocFull), and the search-backed fix
is a deferred engineering follow-up (SI).
