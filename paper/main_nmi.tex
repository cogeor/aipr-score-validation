\section*{Data availability}
The data supporting the findings of this study are openly available at
\url{https://github.com/cogeor/aipr-score-validation}, anchored to the pre-registration tag
\texttt{prereg-iclr2026-v2}. The release contains the two-table contract every analysis
reads: per-submission labels (decision tier, mean reviewer rating) and per-grading-run
scores, keyed by public OpenReview submission identifiers. All decision outcomes, ratings,
and review excerpts derive from the public OpenReview record of ICLR~2026
(\texttt{ICLR.cc/2026/Conference}) under CC~BY~4.0; we redistribute only derived metadata and
our own scores, not manuscript PDFs or full review texts. The full AIPR review text for
identifiable submissions is a controlled audit artifact available to editors and reviewers on
request.

\section*{Code availability}
All analysis code needed to reproduce the study is openly available at
\url{https://github.com/cogeor/aipr-score-validation}; a single command regenerates every
figure, table, and statistic in this paper and its Supplementary Information from the released
data. The manuscript-to-score function evaluated here---AIPR's grading rubric, prompts, and
model configuration---is a proprietary production system and is not released as source.
Consistent with its role as the validated instrument, it is audited through its outputs and
can be exercised on new manuscripts via \url{https://aipr.pub}, with the rubric, prompts, and
configuration made available confidentially to editors, reviewers, or auditors on request.
Because the analysis plan was pre-registered before unblinding, this arrangement lets the
validation be checked independently even though the scorer itself is closed.

\section*{Competing interests}
The author founded and operates AIPR (\url{https://aipr.pub}), the commercial system evaluated
in this study, and stands to benefit from a favourable result. The study was self-funded; no
external funding was received. The mitigations are structural rather than rhetorical: the
analysis plan was frozen at a public tag before any score was joined to an outcome, every
reported number regenerates from released code and data, and the primary comparison includes
a baseline designed to make the pipeline redundant.
